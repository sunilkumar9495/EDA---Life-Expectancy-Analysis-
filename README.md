# Life Expectancy Analysis Using WHO Dataset

## Project Overview

Life expectancy is one of the most important indicators of a country's overall health and development. This project focuses on performing Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on the World Health Organization (WHO) Life Expectancy dataset to identify the key factors that influence life expectancy across different countries.

The objective of this project is to analyze demographic, economic, and healthcare-related variables and understand their relationship with life expectancy. Through data cleaning, visualization, and statistical analysis, meaningful insights are generated to help understand global health trends.

---

## Problem Statement

Various factors such as healthcare expenditure, immunization rates, GDP, education, disease prevalence, and mortality rates impact life expectancy. Understanding these relationships can help governments and healthcare organizations make informed decisions to improve public health outcomes.

This project aims to answer questions such as:

* Which factors have the strongest impact on life expectancy?
* How do developed and developing countries differ in terms of life expectancy?
* What role do healthcare and economic indicators play in increasing life expectancy?
* Which variables are positively or negatively correlated with life expectancy?

## Key Insights

### Positive Factors Affecting Life Expectancy

* Higher Schooling levels are associated with increased life expectancy.
* Countries with higher GDP generally show better life expectancy.
* Improved immunization coverage contributes positively to public health.
* Better healthcare expenditure often correlates with longer life expectancy.

### Negative Factors Affecting Life Expectancy

* Higher Adult Mortality significantly reduces life expectancy.
* HIV/AIDS prevalence negatively impacts lifespan.
* High infant and under-five mortality rates are associated with lower life expectancy.

### Developed vs Developing Countries

* Developed countries generally exhibit higher life expectancy due to better healthcare systems, education, and economic conditions.
* Developing countries show greater variability in health outcomes and mortality rates.
